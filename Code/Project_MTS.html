Overview

Design brief

Create a fresh e-commerce experience for Magic Toy Shop (MTS), a toy store that offers a highly-curated inventory focusing on hand-picked quality over quantity.

The challenges

  • Client did not want a wizard-like product finder, therefore stressing on a superior navigation scheme
  • Duration of 2 weeks
  • Creating a consistent navigation experience while maintaining the feeling of a nostalgic toy shop
  • Lack of access to real users for testing. We were provided personas instead. (This was a hypothetical project at General Assembly)

The insights

  • As MTS is a curation service, user reviews should not be provided as it leads to a conflict on source of information on quality
  • Shoppers might want to browse toys individually or even by collection. Both types of purchase and browsing options should be created
  • Customer help should be easily accessible and easy to use

Our solutions

  • Created a clickable wireframe prototype
  • Grouped items as collections for intuitive search of toys and games
  • Easy customer service access
  • Main navigation bar has product categories as well as curation highlights
  • Easy checkout process

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

To get a sense of how a nostalgia toy shop is, I visited the Toy Collective at Wheaton, Washington D.C. By exploring the shop, I discovered that the most striking aspect of the shop is suddenly coming across toys and games that I had long forgotten or didnâ€™t know existed.

Who exactly is our competition?

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS


The obvious competition to Magic Toy shop are other sources for toys/games (amazon), hobby sites (thinkgeek) and other sites with special hits (etsy). At the same time, it is important to study other e-commerce sites that sell curated goods (Joss & Main).

    Learnings from e-commerce:
  • Easy checkout process
  • Crowd-sourced user reviews
  • Wizard-like search features
    Learnings from hobby sites:
  • Show how things work/look in real life
  • Create collections of different products
  • Remember purchase and suggest re-supplies (especially for magic tricks)
    Learnings from curated e-commerce sites:
  • No user reviews
  • Create urgency through limited time sales
  • Constantly show what is new on their site

Ideation

What do we build?

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION

I got a long list of preferred features from the personas and also learnt a lot from visiting the toy shop as well as analyzing the competition.

Out of these multiple features, I synthesized these top-level qualities that the site should posses:

  • Efficient
  • Personalized
  • Sense of touch and feel
  • Authentic
  • Delightful randomness

Of categories and sub-categories

CARD SORTING

All of us who were chose the Magic Toy Shop project got together to carry out a card-sorting exercise with a list of 100 toys/games/magic tricks we had been given.

We arrived at multiple sub-categories which I aggregated into the following:

  • Toys
  • Games
  • Magic
  • Learning
I also added three top navigation buttons based on the discovery to help users navigate better and help curators highlight their pieces better:
  • Vintage
  • New Arrivals
  • Random toy

*Click *Click *Click

CLICKABLE PROTOTYPE

I started with creating the prototype on Axure, but later shifted to Omnigraffle so as to have greater ease in creating the specification document as well on Omnigraffle. The clickable prototype was made on invision.

I tested three tasks for users to carry out: Purchase a collection, Purchase a specific magic trick and Search for a product youâ€™d like to purchase it.
I learnt that users would want to:

  • Easily reach out to customer service
  • Search within categories
  • Save their payment information for future use
  • Get product suggestions at the bottom

I made the required changes and updated the prototype.
